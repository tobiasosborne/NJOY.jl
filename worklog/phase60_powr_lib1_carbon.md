## Algorithm (carbon path through fast())

1. Parse cards 3-5 for the carbon material entry.
2. Walk the GENDF (`_powr_read_gendf_for_fast`) and capture per-MT
   per-group `(flux, xs)` tuples at temp ≈ rtemp.
3. Compute coarse-group flux: `cflux[jg] = Σ_{ig→jg} flux(ig)`. With
   ngn=68=ngnd and `iclaps=1`, the icgrp map is the identity, so
   `jg = ngnd - ig + 1` (Fortran's group-reversal convention).
4. Invert: `cflux[jg] := 1 / cflux[jg]` (Fortran gamxs.f90:1112-1115).
5. For each MT in [102, 150]: accumulate
   `absxs[jg] += xs(ig) * flux(ig) * cflux[jg]` (gamxs.f90:1156-1163).
6. Write 4 header lines (nid/iwa/iwf/iwr; description; xlol(3..) zeros;
   xlol(1..) zeros) + 12 data lines of 6×e12.5 absorption values.

## Three quirks worth pinning

- **nid = iza × 10** (gamll.f90:828). For carbon with iza=6000 → nid=60000.
- **Group reversal**: jg=1 in the output corresponds to the HIGHEST-energy
  fine group (ig=ngn). Output position 1 = ig=68 (top of GAM-I structure
  ≈ 17 MeV) → 167 mb absorption (MT=107 (n,α) dominant at high E for C).
- **e12.5 with `1p`** has 1 leading digit (1-9) before the decimal, so the
  field is " ±D.DDDDDE±NN" (12 chars). `%E` in C/Julia matches this when
  the leading "+" is replaced with a space. Helper `_powr_e125` handles it.
